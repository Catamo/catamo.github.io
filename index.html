Past

I started programming in the university, feeling like a hacker building Console applications with Pascal and C++, and thinking I was a pro as I used forms with C# and Java. Until Web Development attacked and shot me down of my cloud, I struggled with PHP, HTML, CSS and JS but finally managed to learn enough to approve the class and move on.

In the final semesters I started an internship in a local software development company. I ended up working with them for another one and a half year after graduated. They used the Microsoft stack. I developed in Desktop platform using Winform and WPF, using WCF services for the business layers. All fun and rainbows, until I came face to face with Web Development again. Said to myself that I had to get better in it, and I did. So much that from then my projects were web using ASP.NET MVC mainly.

I learned my way into performant JS, mastered CSS and forgot PHP in favor of ASP.NET and the comfiness of coding on Visual Studio. So I took my honed abilities and got another job with an outsourcing company. Participated in many projects and learnt a great deal along the way.

Present

Currently I am trying to step up my game and be a better software engineer. And what better way to do it than being a founder? Working for others helped me got the experiences and knowledge that I have today, but to me the logical next step is to make my own thing, and make it big.

Yeah I know that its gonna be hard, and that's what thrills me. If you want to check out my struggles, ideas and development progress please go to Chvre.co. There I will work using many tools and technologies and developing many types of projects, searching the one that will take me into the fabled Founders Hall. See you there.
